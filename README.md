Training My Own AI Builder- Part 2: Fine-Tuning an LLM to Become a Personal AI Engineering Assistant

Recently I started experimenting with an idea that I believe will become very important in the future of AI development.

Instead of relying only on general-purpose LLMs, what if we train our own model using our personal knowledge base, engineering notes, and best practices?

The goal is not just to answer questions.
The goal is to create a personal AI engineering assistant that understands how you build systems.
This experiment is part of a larger vision I’m exploring.

I want to build an AI model that can eventually plug directly into my development pipeline and act as a builder assistant (Full explanation in the article).

Not just a chatbot.

But something that can help with:
• designing AI systems
 • guiding coding agents
 • suggesting best practices
 • structuring architectures
 • helping build personalized AI agents for clients

All from prompts.
